# Microsoft Agentic AI Weekly - Brand Brief

## 1) Purpose
`Microsoft Agentic AI Weekly` is a weekly intelligence briefing for teams building and operating Microsoft-centric agent systems.

It is not a link dump. It is a fast decision aid.

## 2) Positioning
We publish high-signal updates that help readers decide what is worth reading deeper.

Positioning line:
`A weekly Microsoft-first briefing with clear summaries, credible sources, and practical signal.`

## 3) Coverage boundaries
Include:
- Microsoft platform updates with implementation impact (Copilot Studio, M365, Foundry, Azure AI)
- Adjacent ecosystem signals only when directly relevant to Microsoft delivery decisions
- Field lessons, patterns, and failures with reproducible context

Exclude by default:
- generic AI trend news without implementation relevance
- promotional announcements with no concrete detail
- claims with weak or missing source evidence

## 4) Voice and editorial style
Voice must be:
- clear
- concise
- neutral but sharp
- evidence-first

Do not use:
- hype language
- vague urgency
- persona-based directives (for example: "If you are a platform owner, you should...")

## 5) Canonical story unit
Each selected item must follow:
1. `Signal`: what happened
2. `Mini-abstract`: compact summary in plain language
3. `Why click`: why the source is worth opening
4. `Source confidence`: official | reputable community | early signal

## 6) Anti-slop rules
Hard rules:
- max 2 sentences in mini-abstract
- max 1 sentence in why-click line
- no repeated boilerplate across items
- no unsupported assertions
- every item must include canonical URL, publisher, and published date

Banned filler examples:
- "It still adds practical context"
- "Use it to tighten decisions" without specifics
- "Useful if this area is in your scope"

## 7) Approval-first policy
Human approval is mandatory before:
1. publishing any website issue
2. sending any newsletter email
3. adding or removing tracked sources

## 8) Success criteria
- Readers can understand each item in under 20 seconds.
- Readers can decide whether to click without reading long paragraphs.
- Weekly issues feel distinct, not templated.
- Trust is reinforced by provenance and confidence labeling.
